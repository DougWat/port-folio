I've always found it fascinating how films like WALL·E and games like Portal 2 bring so much human personality to distinctly non-human characters.

I wanted to try to replicate that effect in Unreal Engine 4 as an exercise, but it ended up becoming a much more involved project that I hope to expand on.

Take a look at the most recent render below.

The Goal

To take a geometric primitive, and program it in such a way that it creates the illusion of life, and more ideally, the appearance of personality. I want to make you sympathize with a herd of cubes.

A Study of Personality

(And the Importance of Eyes)

Much of human communication is non-verbal, and two of the most important players in non-verbal communication are the brow and the eyes. You can detect entire emotional profiles by simply observing those two regions.

While dynamic eyes are all well and good, the vehicle to present all of this is dynamic movement. Idle wandering and responses to stimuli will make these creatures believable. They should run away from things that scare them, move towards things they like, and form groups amongst themselves.

So I took a look at these guys...

Which are fantastic examples of creating expression and personality in non-human characters. WALL·E appears to use head motion and rotating eyes to produce expression, while Portal combines the subtlety of both the head and the eye into a singular unit. Portal also utilizes a dynamic pupil to accent expressions like fear or pain

The Three Heavy Hitters

Gaze

A shifting gaze help's sell the illusion of a living creature. It can create a sense of direction or curiosity.

Pupils

Pupils can be used to convey more complex emotions when no other features are present. Fear can be shown through contraction, while comfort can be shown through dilation.

Blinking

Blinking in responses to stimuli can give the character presence. It's a simple action that can be paired with anything to great effect.
