Evening Forest

Unreal Engine 4 • C++ • Maya

Lets do something zen. I wanted to experiment with color scheming and particle effects in Unreal Engine 4. A forest at the crest of dusk seemed perfect for creating a beautiful color palate.

But this kinda project is one better seen. Take a look at the render below.

I started with a color scheme

I built this scene from an initial color scheme. After scouring some color reference sites and playing around with some combinations, I decided on the above, offering a dark, yet vibrant pallet to draw from.

My Inspiration Board

A desire to explore

A good scene is structured in a way that leads your eyes through it. This can be accomplished with color, light, geometry, or implied motion (in this case, actual motion).

I wanted to instill in the viewer the desire to move into and beyond the presented scene; an urge to explore. In the context of a videogame, this desire is doubly important.

Because of this, all elements in this scene are designed to draw the viewer inward.

Color

The colors transition from a cool deep blue at the edges to a warm pink in the center. This draws the eye through the scene into the middle, but so far this only implied two dimensional movement. We need somthing else

Geometry

The trunks of the trees are likely the first element of the scene the viewer notices. These draw the eye down, reaching and following the horizontal span of grass extending neatly to the center, while the fog implies depth. Cattails draw the eye upwards if viewed from the bottom.

Movement

Subtle, but an essential detail. Moving elements draw you mentally into the scene. Fireflies drift into the forest, giving the viewer's eye a clear element to follow inwards. Ripples in the pond reinforce the critical path. All other movement sells the scene as a living place.
